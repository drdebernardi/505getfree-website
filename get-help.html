What We Help With

Whatever you need,
we'll help you find it.

505 GET FREE connects you with local providers across New Mexico. One call starts the thread — whether you need shelter tonight, legal advice next week, or just someone to talk to.

— i.

Safe Housing

Emergency shelter, transitional housing, and rapid rehousing — for adults, youth, and families.

— ii.

Medical & Mental Health

Trauma-informed care, crisis counseling, and connections to health services that honor your story.

— iii.

Legal Support

Victim advocacy, immigration relief including T-visas and U-visas, and survivor rights counsel.

— iv.

Employment & Income

Job training, financial assistance, benefits navigation, and long-term economic stability support.

— v.

Family Services

Child reunification, family support, and childcare — navigated with care and without judgment.

— vi.

Safety Planning

Personalized safety plans, relocation support, and help obtaining protective orders when needed.

What to Expect

When you call,
this is what happens.

No scripts. No intake forms before you're ready. A real conversation, at your pace.

  1. A trained advocate answers

    A real person picks up — no robots, no voicemail, no holding queue.

  2. You share only what you want

    As much or as little. You're in control of the conversation.

  3. We identify resources near you

    Shelters, attorneys, counselors, tribal advocates — whatever fits your situation, wherever you are.

  4. We connect you with providers

    Warm handoffs where possible. You never have to start your story over.

  5. Follow-up support, when helpful

    Call back any time. We remember you. The thread doesn't break.

Privacy Matters

Your call stays between us.

Confidentiality is at the heart of how we work. You can be anonymous if you want to be. Your information is not shared unless you ask us to share it.

✓

We do not report your call to law enforcement unless you ask us to.

✓

Worried About Someone?

You don't need to be certain to reach out.

Trust your instincts. If something feels wrong, call. An advocate can help you understand what you're seeing and what to do next.
